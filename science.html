Glassy Boron
Agents

A critical ingredient in our extinguishing formula is Glassy Boron, an innovative substance known for its ability to smother and seal fuel sources from oxygen. When deployed, Glassy Boron creates a non-flammable barrier over the fuel, effectively preventing the fire from spreading. This chemical barrier not only suppresses existing flames but also prevents re-ignition by isolating the fuel from potential sparks or heat sources.

Leidenfrost
Effect

HALT's approach to combating intense heat involves a clever application of the Leidenfrost Effect, where our specially formulated wetting agents are designed to penetrate the steam layer that fire generates. This allows the agent to cool the burning material more efficiently than water or traditional foam. By breaking through this thermal barrier, our extinguisher rapidly cools down the fire's core temperature.

Expert Reviewed

Reviewed by Fire Safety Professionals

HALT's formula, packaging, and performance claims have been validated by NSF-registered chemists, NFPA-certified fire safety engineers, and independent third-party testing laboratories.

HALT is developed and manufactured by Prime Safety US Inc., a U.S.-based fire safety manufacturer. Our chemistry team holds active NSF Registration and USDA certification (continuously renewed since 2016), which requires annual submission of batch samples and ingredient disclosures to NSF-approved laboratories for food-contact compliance review.

Every production run is subjected to two independent test protocols before release. The first is an internal quality-control burn test performed on Class A and Class B fuel pans per internal SOP, verifying knockdown time, coverage, and post-extinguishment reignition resistance. The second is a periodic external validation performed at Southwest Research Institute (SwRI), an NFPA/OSHA-recognized independent testing facility, where HALT is tested under the wetting-agent performance protocols referenced in NFPA 18 and evaluated per UL 711 for fire-extinguisher rating classification.

Claims related to environmental safety, PFAS-free composition, and biodegradability are validated by GreenScreen Certified® GFFF (Green Fire Fighting Foam) and listed with the Organic Materials Research Institute (OMRI) for use in USDA National Organic Program environments. Marketing and product claims on this site reflect the specific conditions tested by each of these bodies; certificates of conformity are available on request for retail and institutional buyers via our retail partnerships page.

Full Certification Breakdown

Every certification HALT carries, what the testing process actually measured, and why it matters for fire safety.

NSF & USDA
Food-Contact Safety · Registered Since 2016

The National Sanitation Foundation (NSF) is the public health and safety organization that sets the standards used by the FDA, USDA, and state agencies for products that may contact food, water, or consumer surfaces. HALT has held an active NSF Registration and USDA certification since 2016. The certification process required full ingredient disclosure, toxicological review of every component, and ongoing annual audits of production batches. The NSF mark on HALT means the formula is verified to have zero incidental food-contact hazard — critical for kitchen use, where a fire extinguisher may be deployed over cutting boards, countertops, and open food.

SwRI
Southwest Research Institute · Independent Testing

Southwest Research Institute is one of the largest independent, nonprofit applied research and development organizations in the United States, with an NFPA/OSHA-approved fire testing facility. HALT was submitted to SwRI for independent wetting-agent performance testing under controlled burn conditions. SwRI's protocol measures film formation time, surface tension reduction, vapor suppression, and post-extinguishment reignition resistance. SwRI testing is required by UL for fire extinguisher recognition — results confirm that HALT meets performance benchmarks equivalent to or exceeding traditional AFFF formulations, without the PFAS chemistry.

NFPA 18
National Fire Protection Association · Wetting Agents Standard

NFPA 18 is the Standard on Wetting Agents issued by the National Fire Protection Association — the authoritative body behind nearly every fire code in the United States. The standard defines physical and chemical requirements for wetting agents used in fire suppression: surface tension reduction thresholds, foam expansion ratios, biodegradability baselines, and corrosion-resistance limits. HALT's formula has been tested against the full NFPA 18 protocol and verified to meet or exceed every requirement. NFPA 18 compliance is what allows HALT to be recommended alongside traditional fire suppression systems in residential and commercial guidance.

UL 711
Underwriters Laboratories · Fire Extinguisher Rating Standard

UL 711 is the Underwriters Laboratories standard that defines the fire ratings you see printed on extinguishers (2A:10B:C, etc.). UL testing involves standardized burn pans of specific fuels and sizes, and measures how much fire an agent can extinguish before failing. HALT has passed UL 711 testing protocols, placing it in the same performance tier as traditional portable extinguishers — with the advantage of operating at low aerosol pressure rather than the 100–175 PSI required by stored-pressure dry chemical units. UL is the gold standard in product safety testing; a UL-listed product has passed the same testing rigor used by fire marshals and insurers.

GreenScreen
Certified GFFF · Green Fire Fighting Foam

GreenScreen Certified® for Firefighting Foam (GFFF) is an independent certification program that evaluates fire-fighting foams for human and environmental health hazards. The certification is issued by Clean Production Action, a nonprofit chemistry-assessment body. HALT was submitted for the full GreenScreen List Translator and Hazard Assessment protocol — every ingredient is screened against 18 hazard endpoints including carcinogenicity, endocrine disruption, aquatic toxicity, and environmental persistence. HALT's GFFF certification confirms the formula contains no intentionally-added PFAS, no fluorine, and no chemicals classified as high-hazard under the GreenScreen framework.

OMRI
Organic Materials Research Institute · USDA NOP Listed

OMRI is the independent nonprofit that reviews products for use in USDA National Organic Program (NOP) certified organic operations. An OMRI listing means a product has been verified to contain no ingredients prohibited under the federal organic regulations. HALT's OMRI listing allows the product to be used in certified organic farms, orchards, greenhouses, and food-processing facilities without jeopardizing organic certification. This is particularly relevant for agricultural operations that need fire protection on tractors, barns, and machinery that operate around organic crops.
